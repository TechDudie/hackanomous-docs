# Hackanomous FAQ

Welcome to Hackanomous! If your question isn't answered here, feel free to ask in **#hackanomous-help**.

---

# General info

## What is Hackanomous?

Hackanomous (#hackanomous, https://hackanomous.vercel.app/) is a Hack Club YSWS where you build your AI skills, make something awesome to show off to your family and friends, and win some AI credits, and even more along the way.

Submit any AI or ML related project at all (subject to our Terms & Conditions) and get a pile of stickers, credits, or just make something awesome and win big.

Sounds fun? Join now!

## How long will Hackanomous run for?

Hackanomous' end date is to be confirmed, but we can assure you that it will run for at least three months. This is subject to change.

# Time tracking

## How do I track my time spent coding/designing?

Tracking the time you spend coding or designing your projects is very important for project reviews and bolt payouts.

You will need to track your time using **Hackatime**, a free and open-source tool made for Hack Club members. It measures the amount of time you spend working on your project.

See **#hackatime-help** for more information.

### What if my editor isn't supported?

You can use **Lapse**, which is part of Hackatime. Please check whether your editor is supported before using it.

# Submitting your projects and getting Bolts

## How do I submit my project?

Submitting your project shares it with our reviewers. They'll review your work, leave feedback, and, if approved, trigger your Bolt payout.

## When will I get my Bolts?

You will receive your Bolts once your project has been approved.

The number of Bolts you receive depends on both the quality of your project and the amount of work you have put into it.

## What can I spend my Bolts on?

You can spend Bolts in two ways.

The first is **hardware funding**. When creating a hardware project, you can use Bolts towards grants that help pay for components.

The second is the **Hackanomous Shop**, where you can redeem Bolts for AI credits, stickers, hardware, and other rewards. If you save your Bolts, you can also work towards larger rewards.

If you have any questions about the shop or your Bolt payout, please ask in **#channel**.

To gain access to the rest of the community, open your conversation with **Slackbot**, scroll to the top of the messages, read the instructions, and complete the onboarding process.

# Rules and restrictions

## Who can take part?

Like other Hack Club events, Hackanomous is open to teenagers aged **13–18**.

To prevent abuse, participants must verify their age using either a student ID or government-issued ID before prizes can be shipped. This process is handled by Hack Club.

## What kinds of projects can I make?

Any AI or machine learning related project is welcome.

Projects may be software, hardware, or something completely different, provided AI or ML is a significant part of the project.

Please remember that **vibe coding is not allowed**. Your project must be your own work, with any AI usage clearly declared and only used to assist your development or design process.

Hardware and software projects are reviewed differently due to the different levels of effort involved.

If you have questions, ask in **#channel**.

## Can I also submit my project to other Hack Club programs?

No.

Hackanomous does **not** allow double-dipping. Projects submitted to Hackanomous cannot also be submitted to another Hack Club program that provides rewards.

## Can I collaborate with other people?

Yes.

You may work in groups of **up to five people**.

If everyone is submitting to Hackanomous:

- Each contributor must have their own commits and Hackatime hours reflecting the work they personally completed.
- Each contributor must submit their own project under their own Hackanomous account.
- Clearly mention everyone in the team and, if possible, describe each person's contribution.

## Can I use AI?

Yes.

AI may be used to assist with programming or design, provided you are transparent about how it was used.

Projects created almost entirely by AI tools will not be accepted. We want participants to learn, experiment, and build their own work.

Projects have a maximum of **30% AI usage**.

# Orders

## Will I need to pay shipping costs?

No.

Hack Club covers international shipping costs for prizes.

## Will I need to pay customs fees?

It depends on the item and your country.

Please check the shop listing or ask in our help channel if you're unsure.

## How long do items take to arrive?

Items can take up to **one month** to arrive, depending on the item and where it is being shipped from.

If you have questions about your order, please ask in the help channel.

## Can I track my order?

It depends on the item.

- Stickers and similar letter-mail items are sent using standard post and do not include tracking.
- Larger items generally include tracking information once dispatched.

If your tracking information isn't working, ask in the help channel.

# Helping behind the scenes

## How can I help Hackanomous?

Aside from building amazing projects, the best way to help is by telling other people about Hackanomous, both online and in person.

## Can I help others in #channel?

Absolutely.

Please read this FAQ before answering questions, as it is considered the source of truth.

If you're unsure about something, it's better to check first than accidentally provide incorrect information.

You may also tag **@user**, **@user**, or **@user** (the organisers) **only** if someone has been waiting longer than 24 hours for help in **#channel**.

## How do I join the support team?

Help people in **#channel**.

Helpful community members are regularly noticed and may be invited to join the support team.

## I found a bug!

Please report it in **#channel**.

If possible, include:

- Steps to reproduce the issue
- Expected behaviour
- Actual behaviour
- Screenshots or recordings

The team will investigate and create an issue if necessary.

We will also try to reward useful bug reports with a grant.

## Can I ping the organisers?

Yes, but only if:

- You are reporting a bug, or
- You have been waiting more than **24 hours** for help.

Please remember that organisers and reviewers are volunteers with different time zones and schedules.

## Can I join the Fraud Team?

No.

Due to the nature of its work, the Fraud Team is invite-only and requires a very high level of trust.
