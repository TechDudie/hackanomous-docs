# Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to the Hackanomous FAQ!

If your question is not answered here, ask in the Hackanomous community support channels.

---

## General Information

### What is Hackanomous?

Hackanomous is a Hack Club **You Ship, We Ship (YSWS)** programme where teenagers build and ship AI-powered projects.

The goal is simple: create something awesome using AI or machine learning, learn new skills, and share your work with the world.

You can build:

- Software projects
- Hardware projects
- AI or ML experiments
- Tools that solve real-world problems
- Anything else that uses AI in an interesting way

After completing and submitting your project, you can earn Bolts which can be exchanged for rewards such as AI credits, hardware, development resources, and other prizes.

Hackanomous is about learning through building. You do not need to already be an AI expert — the best way to learn is by experimenting and creating.

---

### Who can participate?

Hackanomous is open to teenagers aged **13–18 years old**.

Age verification may be required before receiving prizes. This helps keep Hack Club programmes safe and ensures everyone is eligible.

---

### Is Hackanomous free?

Yes. Hackanomous is completely free to participate in.

Rewards are funded through sponsors and Hack Club.

Some international shipments may have customs requirements depending on your country.

---

### How long will Hackanomous run for?

The exact end date is currently to be confirmed.

The programme is planned to run for at least several months, giving participants enough time to design, build, and ship meaningful projects.

# Projects

## What projects can I make?

Almost anything involving AI or machine learning!

Projects can be either:

- Software
- Hardware
- A combination of both

Examples include:

- AI-powered applications
- Machine learning experiments
- Robotics projects
- Smart devices
- AI tools
- Creative projects using AI

The most important thing is that your project should solve a problem, explore an idea, or demonstrate something interesting.

---

## Can I use AI to make my project?

Yes, but AI should be used as a tool to help you build — not to completely replace your work.

You should:

- Understand what your project does
- Be transparent about your AI usage
- Explain how AI helped you

Projects that are entirely generated by AI without meaningful contribution from you will not meet the expectations of Hackanomous.

AI usage should stay within the programme guidelines.

---

## Can I collaborate with other people?

Yes!

Projects can be created in teams of up to **5 people**.

If you collaborate:

- Each person should track their own work and time separately
- Each person should clearly explain their contribution
- Each contributor should submit their project under their own account
- Make sure you mention your teammates and what each person worked on

---

## Can I submit my project to other Hack Club programmes?

No.

Hackanomous does not allow double-dipping.

Projects submitted to Hackanomous cannot also be submitted to another Hack Club programme that provides rewards.

---

# Time Tracking

## How do I track my project time?

You must track your building time using **Hackatime**.

Hackatime is free, open-source software built for Hack Clubbers that records the time you spend working on your projects.

Your tracked time helps reviewers understand your effort and project progress.

If your editor is not supported, check whether another supported option such as Lapse is available.

---

# Submitting Projects and Rewards

## How do I submit my project?

When your project is complete, submit it through the Hackanomous submission process.

Your project will then be reviewed by the Hackanomous team.

Reviewers will:

- Look at your project
- Provide feedback
- Evaluate your work
- Approve your Bolts reward if successful

---

## What are Bolts?

Bolts are Hackanomous rewards earned by building and shipping projects.

The number of Bolts you receive depends on factors such as:

- The quality of your project
- The amount of effort involved
- Your creativity
- Your documentation and explanation

---

## What can I spend Bolts on?

Bolts can be used in multiple ways.

You can use them for:

- Hardware project funding
- AI credits
- Stickers and other rewards
- Development resources
- Other items available through the Hackanomous shop

Saving your Bolts may allow you to unlock bigger rewards.

---

# Hardware Projects

## Can I build hardware projects?

Yes!

Hardware projects are encouraged.

Hardware creators may be eligible for funding support to help purchase components and build their ideas.

Hardware and software projects may be reviewed differently because they require different amounts of time and effort.

---

# Shipping and Orders

## Do I need to pay shipping costs?

No.

Hack Club covers shipping costs for prizes.

---

## Will I need to pay customs fees?

This depends on your location and the item being shipped.

Some countries may charge customs fees for imported items.

---

## How long do rewards take to arrive?

Delivery times vary depending on:

- The item
- Your location
- The shipping provider

Some items may take up to a month to arrive.

---

## Can I track my order?

It depends on the item.

- Stickers and smaller mail items are usually sent through regular postal services and may not include tracking.
- Larger items generally include tracking information.

If you have problems with an order, ask for help in the support channels.

---

# Hack Club and Community

## What is Hack Club?

Hack Club is a global community of teenage makers.

It is a nonprofit organisation that helps students learn by building, providing:

- Communities
- Events
- Grants
- Resources
- Opportunities to create amazing projects

---

## How can I help Hackanomous?

The easiest way is to:

- Build great projects
- Share Hackanomous with others
- Help people in the community
- Answer questions when you can

---

## Can I help answer questions?

Yes!

If you help others, make sure you:

- Read the FAQ first
- Check information before sharing it
- Clearly separate facts from personal advice

Incorrect information can cause confusion, so always verify things when unsure.

---

## How do I join the support team?

The best way is to actively help people in the community.

People who consistently provide useful support may be invited to become helpers.

---

## I found a bug. What should I do?

Report bugs in the appropriate support channels.

A good bug report should include:

- What happened
- What you expected to happen
- Steps to reproduce the issue
- Screenshots or recordings if possible

Detailed reports help the team fix problems faster.

---

## Can I contact organisers?

Yes, but please only contact organisers directly when necessary.

Before contacting them:

- Check the FAQ
- Ask in the correct support channels
- Wait for a response if someone is already helping you

Organisers and reviewers may have delays due to time zones, holidays, or other responsibilities.

---

## Can I join the Fraud Team?

No.

The Fraud Team is invite-only because it requires a high level of trust and responsibility.
